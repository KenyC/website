  • A mixed approach to the semantics of "different" (2015)
    (Semantics 1st year squib)

    In this squib, I argue that Charnavel (2015)'s uniform approach to the meanings of "different" can successfully be turned into a movement analysis, where "different" is a scopal element (as in Barker 2007). This move is shown to be crucial if we want to account for readings of "different" under modal predicates. The scopal analysis of "different" also suggests that internal adjectives fall into one of two categories: scopal internal adjectives (like "different") and inert internal adjectives (like "neighboring"). Empirical tests can successfully distinguish the two categories.

  • Compositionality can solve Carnap's problem for natural languages (2015)
    (CogMaster's mini-stage)

    Carnap has shown that one cannot retrieve the reference of logical terms from the patterns of inference they give rise to. Translated to natural languages, this means that functional elements (like every, and) cannot be acquired solely on the basis of entailments between sentences. Bonnay proved that Carnap's problem vanishes if we take compositionality to be a semantic universal (with some other minor assumptions). In this paper, I show that this result extends to the kind of trivalent first-order logic that is suited to deal with presuppositions.

Miscellaneous

Some informal notes about formal language theory:
  • Context-free languages: a constructive characterization (2020)

    In this note, I propose to characterize a constructive characterization of the class of context-free languages. The goal is to have a characterization similar to the characterization of the class of rational languages, as the smallest class of languages closed under some natural operations. The operations proposed are union, concatenation and λ-abstraction and fixed-point. This allows to expand the notion of context-free languages to arbitrary monoids and other weaker structures (e.g. trees). The proposal is illustrated with some Haskell code.

  • Universal languages (2017)

    This is an attempt to elucidate the difference between two different views on the nature of UG: one view according to which UG specifies a class of languages ; one view according to which UG specifies a singe formal language and different mappings to the interface produce different languages. I show that the latter take is only compatible with certain classes of formal languages, such as the class of context-free languages, while certain others, such as the class of mildly context-sensitive languages aren't.

I am the author of the Exh Python PIP package to automate the computation of innocent exclusion exhaustification.
  • Exh for Python

    This Python library can compute the exhaustified meaning of a formula. It computes maximal consistent sets, innocently excludable alternatives, innocently includable alternatives. It can deal with recursive Exhaustified formula and replicates Fox (2007)'s Free Choice account. Install with "pip install exh".
